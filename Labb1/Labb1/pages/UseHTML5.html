Structural HTML5 elements

The <header > elements includes the <nav > elements and the < header > is used to contain the site name and the main navigation. The < nav > element is used to contain the major navigational blocks on the site such as the primary site navigation.

The < footer > elements is located on the bottom of the page and it contains copyright information. It can also contain the adress and links to share the article on social networking sites.

The < article > element represents a section of content that forms an independent part of a document or site; for example, a magazine or newspaper article, or a blog entry

The < section > element groups the related content together, and typically each section would have its own heading.

The < aside > elements has two purposes, depending on whether it is inside an < article > element or not. When it is used inside an < article > it should contain information that is related to the article but not essential to its overall meaning. When the < aside > element is used outside of an <article > element, it acts as a container for content that is related to the entire page.
